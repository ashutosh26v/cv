Research Portfolio

Key Projects

Cleanroom process engineering, thin-film deposition, and semiconductor device characterization — spanning superconducting detectors, infrared photodetectors, and uncooled bolometric sensors.

01
Quantum Sensing · Superconductivity 2024 – 2026

SNSPD NbN Thin-Film Process Development

Reactive sputtering process development for ultra-thin NbN superconducting films, patterned into sub-50 nm meander and spiral nanowires for single-photon detection at cryogenic temperatures.

  • Improved NbN superconducting device Tc from 2.6 K to ~14.5 K through reactive sputtering optimization.
  • Established lithography and RIE process windows for sub-50 nm superconducting nanowires.
  • Correlated sputtering conditions, stoichiometry, and disorder with superconducting device performance.
  • Analyzed switching-current statistics and electrical response of meander and spiral SNSPD geometries.

02
Optoelectronics · Heterojunctions 2024 – 2026

Sb₂Te₃ Heterojunction Photodetectors

Interface-engineered Sb₂Te₃/TiO₂ and Sb₂Te₃/TMDC thin-film stacks enabling self-powered, broadband photodetection from visible to short-wave infrared (400–1700 nm).

  • Integrated Sb₂Te₃/TiO₂ and Sb₂Te₃/TMDC heterostructures for broadband photodetection.
  • Achieved broadband photoresponse from 400 nm to 1700 nm through interface-engineered thin-film stacks.
  • Improved interface quality and reduced dark current through sputtering and annealing optimization.
  • Performed structural, electrical, and optoelectronic characterization of heterojunction devices.

03
Thermal Sensing · Thin-Film Bolometry Dec 2022 – Jan 2023

CuFeSe₂ Thin-Film Bolometric Devices

Sputtered CuFeSe₂ thin-film process development for uncooled infrared bolometric sensing, with thermal isolation engineering for improved device response speed.

  • Optimized sputtered CuFeSe₂ thin-film growth through deposition-temperature engineering.
  • Correlated structural evolution with thermally activated transport and bolometric device performance.
  • Achieved ~3% K⁻¹ negative TCR near room temperature for infrared bolometric sensing.
  • Reduced thermal time constant from 12.18 s to 7.90 s through backside silicon removal and thermal isolation engineering.

04
Infrared Sensing · Nanocrystal Devices 2021 – 2023

CuFeSe₂ Nanocrystal Bolometric Devices

Solution-processed CuFeSe₂ nanocrystal thin films for uncooled bolometric sensing, demonstrating TCR performance competitive with commercial VOx architectures.

  • Developed solution-processed CuFeSe₂ nanocrystal thin films with controlled thickness and film uniformity.
  • Correlated nanocrystal film processing conditions with temperature-dependent electrical transport behavior.
  • Established electrical characterization workflows for extraction of bolometric response and TCR.
  • Demonstrated infrared-induced bolometric response comparable to conventional uncooled thermal sensing architectures.
