About Me

From Atoms to Devices

I'm a PhD researcher at the Centre for Nano Science and Engineering (CeNSE), Indian Institute of Science, Bengaluru, specializing in thin-film process engineering and micro/nanofabrication. My doctoral work focuses on developing superconducting nanowire single-photon detectors (SNSPDs) — devices that operate near absolute zero and count individual photons.

With over 300 sputtering runs and years of cleanroom experience, I bridge the gap between fundamental materials science and practical device performance. I take pride in correlating process parameters with structural and electrical data to establish reproducible, high-yield fabrication windows.

Before research, I worked as a Signal & Telecom Site Engineer with Rail Vikas Nigam Limited, which gave me a strong appreciation for field execution, cross-team coordination, and delivering under strict constraints.

Outside the lab I am active in photography and movie making — currently leading IISc's creative team. This work sharpens my eye for composition, storytelling, and delivering polished output under deadline pressure.
